The Assignment

This document is a Technical Offer in response to the Terms of Reference for a Financial Market Intelligence Expert issued by the Rwanda Mines, Petroleum and Gas Board (RMB).

RMB requires an expert to establish a structured, data-driven mineral financial intelligence function — enabling the Board to monitor global mineral markets, analyse pricing and valuation methodologies, assess trade routes and buyer profiles, track mining finance trends, and produce periodic intelligence reports for senior management and policymakers.

The mandate covers Rwanda's four priority minerals — cassiterite, coltan, wolframite, and gold — in a context of accelerating global demand for critical minerals, increasing regulatory requirements from key buyer markets (EU, US), and an urgent need to transition from artisanal to semi-industrial and industrial mining.

This Technical Offer

This offer proposes an 18-month programme in 4 phases, built on a company-centric financial intelligence philosophy and grounded in Rwanda's data realities. It delivers not only analytical outputs — but a permanent institutional capability that RMB will own and operate independently beyond the engagement.

Phase 1 (M1–M2) Diagnostic, data audit, financial intelligence framework design, and delivery of the first working analytical tool (Current Situation Model). Phase 2 (M3–M6) Pricing reference guide, cost structure models, demand-offer-price dynamic modeling, logistics and policy cost frameworks, first quarterly report. Phase 3 (M7–M12) Automated intelligence dashboard, investor hub, full costs modeling framework, market access cost framework, annual outlook, staff training. Phase 4 (M10–M18) Company portal, multi-market integration model, complete financial flows modeling, full platform launch, regional hub roadmap.

The Bicycle Equilibrium

Market equilibrium is dynamic — like a bicycle: without movement one falls, but excessive speed without control leads to a crash. Mastering it requires knowing the destination, reading the road, and continuously calibrating speed. This is what mineral financial intelligence must do: ride the market — not fall off it.

⚠️ Production-Side Markets — Often Overlooked: Markets also exist on the production side — energy, raw materials, investment capital, machinery — each with their own demand-offer-price dynamics. A disruption in any of these directly impacts production costs and competitive positioning on the selling side.

2 — 3T Minerals Strategy: Financial Models for Productivity, Profitability & Attractiveness

Rwanda is already a world-class 3T producer. The strategic question is no longer "how much do we produce" — but "how much of the value do we capture." Financial intelligence is the tool that answers this question and guides the transition from artisanal (40% extraction efficiency) toward industrial operations (target: 80%).

📈 Financial Models for Productivity Growth
Transition to semi-industrial and industrial operations requires financial modeling of asset investment thresholds, energy consumption profiles, and return on investment per mechanisation level. RMB-FinIntel provides the analytical framework to sequence investments and assess viability at company level.
💰 Profitability at Company Level
Moving from sector-level revenue statistics to company-level profitability analysis. Which companies are structurally profitable? Which are surviving on prefinancing? Which have the capacity to upgrade operations? These questions cannot be answered without company-centric financial intelligence — and cannot be ignored if Rwanda wants to attract serious international capital.
🌍 Attracting International Operators
Rio Tinto's joint venture with the Government of Rwanda — registered as Nyabarongo Mining & Exploration Ltd (Rio Tinto 75% / GoR 25%) — covers exploration of lithium, tin, tantalum, tungsten and associated metals. Announced by RDB in 2024, confirmed by Rio Tinto on their own Africa operations page. This JV is a template for the level of international partnership Rwanda can attract. To replicate and scale it, Rwanda must offer the data infrastructure that sophisticated investors expect: verified geological data, transparent cost benchmarks, reliable regulatory financial intelligence, and a credible pricing reference system.

3 — Designing the Financial Ecosystem: Roles of Key Institutions

Rwanda's ambition to become a regional mineral trading hub requires a coordinated financial ecosystem. Each institution plays a distinct and complementary role — and none can succeed in isolation. Financial intelligence is the connective tissue that makes this ecosystem function.

🏦
Commercial Banks
Working capital, trade finance, and prefinancing alternatives that protect exporters' negotiating position. Currently underserved due to lack of financial transparency in the sector. RMB-FinIntel credit risk profiles would unlock commercial bank lending at scale and reduce exporters' dependency on foreign prefinancers.
🏛️
KIFC — Kigali International Finance Centre
Positioning Kigali as the structuring hub for African minerals investment funds, offtake financing vehicles, and commodity trading entities. KIFC's incentives and cross-border transaction infrastructure are powerful attractors — but require credible financial intelligence data to attract sophisticated fund managers and international commodity traders.
📊
CMA — Capital Market Authority
Overseeing capital market instruments for minerals sector financing — bonds, structured products, and under the Draft Virtual Assets Law, the tokenisation of mineral assets. CMA is the designated regulatory authority for virtual asset service providers and real-world asset tokenisation. Coordination with RMB on mineral pricing intelligence is essential.
🏧
BNR — National Bank of Rwanda
As of July 2025, BNR has officially begun building gold as a national reserve asset — joining Uganda, Tanzania, and Kenya. This creates a direct institutional linkage between the financial intelligence platform and the national gold strategy: real-time gold valuation and market intelligence become a central bank requirement, not merely a sector tool.

4 — Trading Empowerment: Breaking the Prefinancing Trap

One of the most significant — and least visible — mechanisms through which value leaks from Rwanda's mineral sector is prefinancing. Addressing it requires sustained analytical work at company level and a multi-stakeholder response.

⚠️ The Prefinancing Trap

Prefinancing — where international buyers advance funds to Rwandan exporters before delivery — creates a dependency that systematically suppresses export prices. The financer becomes simultaneously the buyer, the lender, and the price-setter. The exporter, lacking alternative liquidity, accepts prices below market benchmarks as the implicit cost of the financing. This mechanism is not illegal — but it is a primary driver of the gap between declared export values and international market benchmarks.

📉 Gap Curves by Exporter
Build price gap curves per exporter per mineral over 5–10 years. Track whether each exporter's gap is widening or narrowing. Identify who is systematically trapped in prefinancing dependency and who has successfully diversified their buyer base and financing sources.
📊 Gap Curves by Mineral
Build price gap curves per mineral across all exporters over 5–10 years. Reveal which minerals are most systematically underpriced relative to LME/LBMA benchmarks, and whether gaps are driven by global market dynamics or local market power asymmetries.
🔍 Gap Decomposition
Working with exporters to explain and break down the gap into fee components: treatment charges, refining charges, moisture deductions, quality penalties, logistics costs, and the implicit prefinancing cost. This decomposition makes the gap transparent and actionable — for both individual exporters and for RMB in its regulatory and advisory role.
🤝 Mechanisms to Reduce the Gap
Discuss with all stakeholders — exporters, commercial banks, KIFC, BNR, international buyers — the mechanisms needed: access to local trade finance, collective negotiation frameworks, forward pricing contracts, warehouse receipts, and a transparent mineral pricing reference that shifts negotiating power back toward Rwandan exporters.

5 — National Gold Strategy & Digital Asset Innovation

Gold occupies a unique position in Rwanda's mineral strategy — as both an export commodity and a national reserve asset. The convergence of BNR's gold reserve strategy and Rwanda's draft Virtual Assets Law creates a strategic opportunity without precedent in the region.

🏅
BNR National Gold Reserve Strategy
As of July 2025, BNR has officially begun acquiring gold as a national reserve asset — a historic first for Rwanda, following Tanzania, Uganda and Kenya. This positions RMB-produced gold not only as an export commodity but as a strategic national asset with macroeconomic implications. Financial intelligence linking production data, export pricing, and BNR reserve valuations becomes a cross-institutional requirement.
⚖️
Draft Virtual Assets Law (BNR/CMA, March 2025)
Rwanda's draft law — developed jointly by BNR and CMA, released for public consultation March 2025 — explicitly provides for tokenisation of real-world assets. Requirements: 100% collateralisation, ring-fencing of underlying assets, periodic independent valuation by an accredited authority, and verifiable issuer ownership documentation. CMA is designated as the Regulatory Authority for virtual asset service providers.
🔗 Concrete Recommendation — Mineral Tokenisation Pilot Programme

We recommend that RMB, in close coordination with BNR, CMA, KIFC, and selected mining companies, initiates a Mineral Tokenisation Pilot Programme — beginning with gold as the most liquid, internationally benchmarked, and strategically significant mineral for Rwanda's national reserves strategy.

Tokenisation of physically-backed Rwandan gold would enable: (1) fractional ownership of certified gold for domestic and international investors through KIFC-registered digital platforms; (2) transparent, real-time price discovery anchored to LME/LBMA benchmarks; (3) direct linkage between BNR's gold reserve strategy and a digital asset infrastructure; and (4) Rwanda's positioning as Africa's first jurisdiction with a legally regulated, fully collateralised mineral token market.

This is equally a bold strategic opportunity that requires careful preparation and genuine multi-stakeholder alignment — not just regulatory approval. RMB must work hand-in-hand with BNR (gold reserves), CMA (regulatory oversight), KIFC (financial structuring), and mining companies (traceability and collateral). The RMB-FinIntel financial intelligence infrastructure — pricing reference system, valuation methodology, company-level financial data — is a prerequisite for credible tokenisation. The 18-month programme builds the foundation; the tokenisation pilot can realistically follow in Year 3.
